## Overview

The Alerts Engine automatically evaluates your sales data against predefined business rules and flags issues requiring attention. The system generates alerts for underperforming sellers, at-risk clients, stagnant products, and growth opportunities.

<Tip>
Alerts are calculated in real-time based on your current filters. Change your date range or filters to see how alert conditions evolve.
</Tip>

## Alert Severity Levels

Alerts are classified into four severity levels:

| Severity | Icon | Color | Meaning | Response Time |
|----------|------|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Alta** (Critical) | 🔴 | Red | Immediate threat to business | Within 24 hours |
| **Media** (Medium) | 🟡 | Yellow | Developing issue requiring attention | Within 3-5 days |
| **Baja** (Low) | 🔵 | Blue | Minor issue to address | Within 1-2 weeks |
| **Info** (Opportunity) | 🟢 | Green | Positive trend or opportunity | As time permits |

**Reference:** Alert severity definitions in `src/alerts_engine.py:14-71`

## Available Alert Rules

### ALT001: Vendedor bajo rendimiento crítico

<Warning>
**Severity:** Critical (🔴 alta)
</Warning>

**Trigger Condition:**
Seller with cumplimiento < 65% in the last 30 days

**Code Reference:** `src/alerts_engine.py:102-124`

```python
# Condition check
if cumplimiento < 0.65:
    # Alert triggered
```

**What It Means:**
A seller is achieving less than 65% of their monthly target, indicating severe underperformance.

**Suggested Action:**
"Revisar cartera y objetivos con supervisor" (Review portfolio and objectives with supervisor)

<Accordion title="How to Respond to ALT001">
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Immediate Review Meeting">
      Schedule a one-on-one within 24 hours:
      - Review seller's client portfolio
      - Check visit frequency and activity logs
      - Identify if this is a new pattern or recurring issue
      - Assess if targets are realistic given territory
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Investigate Root Causes">
      **Possible causes:**
      - Personal/health issues affecting performance
      - Territory changes or client losses
      - Inadequate training or product knowledge
      - Competitive pressure in territory
      - Unrealistic objectives
      
      **Data to check:**
      - Filter by this seller: Review their client list
      - Check `clientes_perdidos` and `dias_desde_ultima_compra`
      - Compare their margin % with team average
      - Review transaction count vs. team average
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Create Action Plan">
      Develop specific, measurable actions:
      - Daily check-ins for 2 weeks
      - Joint client visits with supervisor
      - Focus on recovering top 5 inactive clients
      - Specific product training if needed
      - Temporary target adjustment if justified
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Mark Alert as Reviewed">
      In the dashboard:
      - Click "✅ Marcar revisada" button
      - Document actions taken in your CRM/notes
      - Set follow-up review date (recommend 1 week)
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Accordion>

### ALT002: Cliente sin compras (riesgo de pérdida)

<Note>
**Severity:** Medium (🟡 media)
</Note>

**Trigger Condition:**
Active client with no purchases in the last 45 days

**Code Reference:** `src/alerts_engine.py:126-145`

```python
# Condition check
if uc is None or uc < (today - 45 days):
    # Alert triggered
```

**What It Means:**
A client marked as active in your system hasn't ordered in over 6 weeks, suggesting they may be at risk of churning to a competitor.

**Suggested Action:**
"Contacto de recuperación urgente" (Urgent recovery contact)

<Accordion title="How to Respond to ALT002">
  **Priority actions:**
  
  1. **Check client's Pareto category:**
     - Navigate to Clientes page
     - Filter to find the specific client
     - Check `categoria_pareto` field
     
  2. **Response based on category:**
     
     **Category A clients:**
     - Immediate phone call from manager (not just seller)
     - In-person visit within 48 hours
     - Investigate: Have they switched suppliers?
     - Offer incentive to place order (if policy allows)
     
     **Category B clients:**
     - Seller phone call within 24 hours
     - Understand reason for inactivity
     - Schedule visit if concern identified
     - Send promotional offer
     
     **Category C clients:**
     - Automated email or lower-priority outreach
     - May not justify intensive recovery effort
     - Consider removing from active list if no response
  
  3. **Document outcome:**
     - What did you learn from the contact?
     - Is the client recoverable?
     - Update client status if they've closed/moved
</Accordion>

### ALT003: Caída de venta en cliente relevante

<Warning>
**Severity:** Critical (🔴 alta)
</Warning>

**Trigger Condition:**
Category A client with sales decline > 25% vs. previous period

**Code Reference:** `src/alerts_engine.py:147-175`

```python
# Condition check
if client in category_A and delta < -0.25:
    # Alert triggered
```

**What It Means:**
One of your most valuable clients (Pareto A) has significantly reduced their purchasing. This is a critical early warning sign.

**Suggested Action:**
"Visita de cuenta clave" (Key account visit)

<Accordion title="How to Respond to ALT003">
  **Immediate actions (same day):**
  
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Gather Intelligence">
      Before contacting the client:
      - Review their complete purchase history
      - Check if decline is across all products or specific categories
      - Review margin trends (are they negotiating harder?)
      - Check if payment issues exist
      - Ask seller if they're aware of any changes
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Executive-Level Contact">
      This requires senior attention:
      - Manager or director makes initial contact
      - Schedule formal account review meeting
      - Prepare value proposition review
      - Be ready to discuss terms, pricing, service
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Conduct Account Review">
      In the meeting:
      - Understand their business changes
      - Ask directly about competition
      - Review their satisfaction with:
        - Product quality
        - Pricing competitiveness
        - Delivery reliability
        - Seller service level
      - Identify opportunities to regain share
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Develop Recovery Plan">
      Based on findings:
      - Address specific concerns raised
      - Consider temporary incentives if appropriate
      - Increase visit frequency
      - Assign additional support resources
      - Set specific recovery targets and timeline
    </Step>
  </Steps>
  
  <Warning>
  **Prevention is better than cure:** Regular business reviews with Pareto A clients prevent surprises. Schedule quarterly reviews with your top 20% clients.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Note>
**Severity:** Low (🔵 baja)

**Trigger Condition:**
Active product with no sales in 60 days

**What It Means:**
A product in your active catalog hasn't sold in 2 months, suggesting it may be obsolete or poorly positioned.

**Suggested Action:**
"Revisar disponibilidad y push comercial" (Review availability and commercial push)

<Accordion title="How to Respond to ALT004">
  **Investigation questions:**
  
  1. **Is this a seasonal product?**
     - Check sales history for same period last year
     - If seasonal, this alert may be normal
     
  2. **Is it in stock?**
     - Verify physical availability
     - Check if listed in current price list
     - Confirm sellers know about it
     
  3. **What's the Pareto category?**
     - Check Productos page
     - If Category C: Consider discontinuation
     - If Category A or B: This is serious
  
  **Actions based on findings:**
  
  <AccordionGroup>
    <Accordion title="Category A Product with No Sales">
      **This is a critical issue:**
      - Immediate investigation required
      - Check for system errors (maybe sales not recorded?)
      - Review competitive situation
      - Launch promotional campaign
      - Train/remind sellers about product
      - Consider pricing adjustment
    </Accordion>
    
    <Accordion title="Category B Product">
      **Moderate concern:**
      - Include in next sales meeting
      - Feature in seller communications
      - Consider bundling with A products
      - Review if competing with better alternative
    </Accordion>
    
    <Accordion title="Category C Product">
      **Low priority:**
      - Evaluate cost of carrying inventory
      - Consider discontinuation
      - If strategic reason to keep, accept low sales
      - Don't invest resources in promoting
    </Accordion>
  </AccordionGroup>
</Accordion>

### ALT005: Vendedor sin actividad reciente

<Note>
**Severity:** Medium (🟡 media)
</Note>

**Trigger Condition:**
Seller with no transactions in the last 7 business days

**Code Reference:** `src/alerts_engine.py:199-217`

```python
# Condition check
if last_activity < today - 7 business days:
    # Alert triggered
```

**What It Means:**
A seller hasn't recorded any sales transactions in over a week of working days.

**Suggested Action:**
"Verificar actividad de campo" (Verify field activity)

<Accordion title="How to Respond to ALT005">
  **Possible explanations:**
  
  - **Legitimate:** Seller on vacation, sick leave, training
  - **Concerning:** Seller working but not closing sales
  - **Critical:** Seller not visiting clients
  
  **Actions to take:**
  
  1. **Immediate check:**
     - Is the seller scheduled to work?
     - Any approved leave?
     - Check if they're submitting visit reports
     
  2. **If seller is working:**
     - Review visit frequency
     - Are they seeing clients but not closing?
     - Pipeline/funnel issue or skill issue?
     - Need help with specific clients?
     
  3. **If activity is low:**
     - Daily check-ins for 1 week
     - Joint visits with manager
     - Review territory routing efficiency
     - Address any personal/motivation issues
  
  <Tip>
  Use this alert as an early warning system. Seven days without sales may indicate a problem that will show up in ALT001 next month if not addressed.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

### ALT006: Oportunidad: cliente B en crecimiento

<Note>
**Severity:** Opportunity (🟢 info)
</Note>

**Trigger Condition:**
Category B client with growth > 30% vs. previous period

**Code Reference:** `src/alerts_engine.py:219-247`

```python
# Condition check
if client in category_B and delta > 0.30:
    # Alert triggered
```

**What It Means:**
A mid-tier client is showing strong growth and may be ready to become a Category A client with proper attention.

**Suggested Action:**
"Potencial para upgrade a categoría A — aumentar frecuencia de visita" (Potential to upgrade to category A — increase visit frequency)

<Accordion title="How to Capitalize on ALT006">
  **This is a positive alert - seize the opportunity:**
  
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Understand the Growth">
      Research why they're growing:
      - Their business is expanding?
      - They reduced purchases from competitors?
      - Specific product driving growth?
      - Seasonal spike or sustained trend?
      
      **Data to check:**
      - Filter to this client on Clientes page
      - Review their product mix
      - Check if frequency or ticket size increased
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Increase Engagement">
      Strike while momentum is positive:
      - Increase visit frequency (weekly if monthly)
      - Involve manager in relationship
      - Introduce expanded product lines
      - Discuss volume-based incentives
      - Ensure excellent service (priority treatment)
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Cross-Sell and Upsell">
      Leverage the positive relationship:
      - Review what they're NOT buying from you
      - Introduce complementary products
      - Offer product training/education
      - Share industry insights and trends
    </Step>
    
    <Step title="Lock in the Growth">
      Make switching costly:
      - Discuss longer-term agreements
      - Integrate systems (if applicable)
      - Build multiple relationships in their organization
      - Deliver exceptional value
    </Step>
  </Steps>
  
  <Tip>
  **Proactive portfolio management:** Category B clients growing to A status is healthier than replacing lost A clients. Nurture your B clients actively.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

### ALT007: Equipo cerca del objetivo mensual

<Note>
**Severity:** Opportunity (🟢 info)
</Note>

**Trigger Condition:**
Team cumplimiento > 85% with more than 5 business days remaining in the month

**Code Reference:** `src/alerts_engine.py:249-270`

```python
# Condition check
if cumplimiento_general > 0.85 and dias_habiles_restantes > 5:
    # Alert triggered
```

**What It Means:**
Your team is on track to meet (and potentially exceed) the monthly objective. A final push can ensure success and potentially achieve outstanding results.

**Suggested Action:**
"Push final para cerrar mes en verde" (Final push to close month in green)

<Accordion title="How to Respond to ALT007">
  **This is your chance to turn good into great:**
  
  **Communication strategy:**
  
  1. **Celebrate current success:**
     - Acknowledge the team is performing well
     - Recognize top performers publicly
     - Create positive momentum
     
  2. **Set stretch goals:**
     - "We're at 87%, let's hit 100%"
     - "Only $X more to achieve target"
     - Make it feel achievable and exciting
     
  3. **Identify quick wins:**
     - Review clients close to ordering
     - Follow up on pending quotes
     - Contact A-clients due for reorder
     - Push any delayed orders to close this month
     
  4. **Incentivize if possible:**
     - Bonus for exceeding 100%?
     - Recognition/prizes for top performers?
     - Team celebration if target met?
  
  **Tactical focus for final days:**
  
  - **Don't:** Chase any sale regardless of margin
  - **Do:** Focus on high-margin opportunities
  - **Don't:** Pressure clients (damages relationships)
  - **Do:** Help clients who were planning to order anyway
  - **Don't:** Offer unsustainable discounts
  - **Do:** Highlight genuine value and urgency
  
  <Warning>
  Beware of "month-end stuffing" where sales are artificially pulled forward, stealing from next month. Focus on sustainable performance.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

## Using the Alerts Dashboard

### Viewing Alerts

Navigate to the **Alertas** page (🚨 Centro de Alertas y Oportunidades)

**Summary cards show:**
- 🔴 Critical alerts count
- 🟡 Medium alerts count
- 🔵 Low alerts count
- 🟢 Opportunities count
- Total alerts count

**Reference:** `pages/5_alertas.py:59-107`

### Filtering Alerts

Use the filter controls to focus on specific alerts:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filter by Severity">
    Select one or more severity levels:
    - Focus on 🔴 Críticas for daily management
    - Review 🟢 Oportunidades weekly
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter by Entity Type">
    Select entity types:
    - `vendedor`: Seller-related alerts
    - `cliente`: Client-related alerts
    - `producto`: Product-related alerts
    - `equipo`: Team-level alerts
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Hide Reviewed Alerts">
    Check "Ocultar revisadas" to hide alerts you've already addressed in this session.
    
    <Note>
    Reviewed status is stored in session state (`st.session_state["alertas_revisadas"]`) and persists during your current session only.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Alert Information Display

Each alert card shows:

- **Alert ID and severity badge:** [ALT001] · ALTA
- **Alert name:** Descriptive title
- **Entity information:** Specific seller/client/product affected
- **Detail:** Current values and context
- **Suggested action:** Recommended response
- **Review status:** Whether you've marked it as reviewed

**Reference:** `pages/5_alertas.py:159-203`

### Managing Alert Actions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Alert Details">
    Read the alert information:
    - Understand what triggered it
    - Note the specific entity (seller name, client name, etc.)
    - Review the current vs. reference values
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Investigate if Needed">
    Use filters to drill down:
    - Apply filters to isolate the entity
    - Navigate to relevant page (Vendedores, Clientes, Productos)
    - Review detailed KPIs for context
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Take Action">
    Follow the suggested action:
    - Schedule meetings
    - Make phone calls
    - Send communications
    - Update priorities
    - Document actions in your CRM/system
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Mark as Reviewed">
    Click **"✅ Marcar revisada"**:
    - Removes alert from default view
    - Helps you track which alerts you've addressed
    - Alert will reappear if condition persists in future sessions
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Alert Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Daily Alert Review Routine">
    **Every morning (5-10 minutes):**
    
    1. Open Alertas page
    2. Check count of critical alerts (🔴)
    3. Review any new critical alerts since yesterday
    4. Assign actions for the day
    5. Mark yesterday's alerts as reviewed if addressed
    
    **Make it a habit:** Alert review should be the first thing you do each morning.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Weekly Alert Analysis">
    **Every Monday (30 minutes):**
    
    1. Review all alert types (not just critical)
    2. Look for patterns:
       - Same seller appearing repeatedly?
       - Multiple clients in same zone at risk?
       - Systematic issues with certain products?
    3. Discuss recurring alerts in team meeting
    4. Implement systemic fixes for patterns
    5. Track week-over-week alert count trends
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Alert Escalation Policy">
    Define clear ownership:
    
    **Critical alerts (🔴):**
    - Seller alerts: Sales manager must act within 24h
    - Client alerts (Pareto A): Sales manager personally involved
    - Escalate to director if no resolution in 1 week
    
    **Medium alerts (🟡):**
    - Assigned to sellers with manager oversight
    - Review in weekly one-on-ones
    - Escalate if becomes critical
    
    **Low alerts (🔵):**
    - Document but lower priority
    - Address in regular workflow
    - May accept some low alerts as normal
    
    **Opportunities (🟢):**
    - Don't ignore these!
    - Assign to top performers
    - Recognize success in capitalizing on opportunities
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Document Your Actions">
    **For each alert you address:**
    
    1. **Record the alert:** Alert ID, date, entity
    2. **Document investigation:** What you found
    3. **Note actions taken:** What you did
    4. **Set follow-up date:** When to re-check
    5. **Track outcome:** Did the action work?
    
    **Why this matters:**
    - Build knowledge base of what works
    - Show clients/sellers you take issues seriously
    - Demonstrate proactive management to leadership
    - Improve alert response over time
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Alert Limitations

<Warning>
**Important considerations:**
</Warning>

### What Alerts Can't Do

1. **No persistence:** Alert review status resets each session. Implement external tracking for continuity.

2. **No notifications:** The system doesn't send emails or push notifications. You must check the dashboard proactively.

3. **Filter dependency:** Alerts recalculate based on active filters. If you filter to one zone, you'll only see alerts for that zone.

4. **No historical tracking:** Can't see that an alert existed yesterday but is resolved today. Consider periodic exports for trends.

5. **Rule-based only:** Alerts follow predefined rules. They won't catch unique situations that don't match the rules.

### When to Override Alert Rules

**Alerts are guidelines, not absolute rules. Override when:**

- **Known temporary situation:** Seller on approved leave (ALT005 false alarm)
- **Seasonal business:** Product with expected dormant periods (ALT004 acceptable)
- **Data lag:** Recent orders not yet in system (apparent inactivity)
- **Strategic decision:** Accepting lower performance for strategic reasons

**Always document why you're overriding an alert decision.**
