## Overview

The Customer Analysis view helps you understand which customers drive your business and where portfolio risks exist. Using Pareto analysis and behavioral metrics, you can prioritize high-value relationships and prevent customer churn.

<Note>
  This view answers the critical question: "Which customers actually sustain our revenue, and are we at risk of losing them?"
</Note>

## Key Metrics

The top KPI cards display portfolio health:

### Active Customers
- Count of unique customers who purchased in the period
- Calculated as `df['id_cliente'].nunique()`
- Coverage % showing active/total portfolio ratio

### Portfolio Coverage
- Percentage of total customer base that purchased
- Formula: `(active_customers / total_customers_in_portfolio) * 100`
- Indicates effectiveness of sales team reach

**Calculated using** `kpi_cobertura_clientes(df, cli_df, df_ant)`

### New Customers
- Customers making their first purchase in this period
- Detected by checking if `id_cliente` exists in `df_ant` (previous period)
- Critical for growth tracking

### Lost Customers  
- Customers who purchased previously but not in current period
- At risk of permanent churn
- Requires immediate re-engagement action

<Warning>
  A high lost customer count combined with low new customer acquisition indicates serious portfolio erosion.
</Warning>

## Pareto Chart

Visualizes the 80/20 principle in your customer base:

- **Blue bars:** Individual customer revenue (sorted descending)
- **Orange line:** Cumulative percentage
- **80% threshold line:** Shows where you reach 80% of total revenue

**Key insights:**
- How many customers make up the top 80% of sales?
- Is revenue concentration healthy or risky?
- Which specific customers are in the critical top tier?

**Generated using** `chart_pareto(df_cli, titulo="Pareto de Clientes", col_nombre="razon_social", col_valor="importe_total", top_n=30)`

<Tip>
  If fewer than 15-20% of customers drive 80% of revenue, your business has healthy diversification. If it's less than 5%, you have concentration risk.
</Tip>

## Pareto Concentration Metrics

Three key metrics assess portfolio health:

### Category A Customers
- Number of customers in top tier (usually ~20% of portfolio)
- Their share of total revenue (target ~80%)
- Shows as both count and percentage

### Revenue from A Customers
- Percentage of total sales from category A
- Benchmark: 70-80% is healthy
- >85% indicates concentration risk
- Less than 70% suggests weak customer differentiation

### Gini Index
- Statistical measure of concentration (0 to 1)
- **0.0** = Perfect equality (all customers buy exactly the same)
- **1.0** = Perfect inequality (one customer is 100% of revenue)
- **Typical healthy range:** 0.35 to 0.55
- **Concentration risk:** >0.65

**Calculated using** `get_concentracion_pareto(kcli, "importe_total")` from `src/pareto.py`

<Note>
  The Gini index helps you monitor concentration trends over time. If it's steadily increasing, you're becoming more dependent on fewer customers.
</Note>

## At-Risk Customer Detection

The system automatically flags customers at risk of churning:

### Risk Criteria

A customer is flagged if **any** of these conditions are met:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Inactivity Period">
    No purchase in the last 30 days: `dias_desde_ultima_compra > 30`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revenue Decline">
    Sales down >30% vs. previous period: `vs_periodo_anterior < -0.30`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Frequency Drop">
    Purchase frequency decreased significantly period-over-period
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Calculation in** `kpi_clientes()` engine:
```python
kcli['flag_en_riesgo'] = (
    (kcli['dias_desde_ultima_compra'] > 30) |
    (kcli['vs_periodo_anterior'].fillna(0) < -0.30)
)
```

### At-Risk Customer List

A dedicated section shows only flagged customers with:
- Customer name and category
- Specific risk factor (inactivity / declining sales)
- Days since last contact
- Assigned salesperson
- Suggested action

<Warning>
  Prioritize at-risk customers in categories A and B — losing a high-value customer has outsized impact on revenue.
</Warning>

## Growth Opportunities

The system identifies customers with high growth potential:

### Opportunity Criteria

Customers flagged as opportunities meet:
- **Pareto category B** (mid-tier, not yet top performers)
- **Sales growth >20%** vs. previous period (`vs_periodo_anterior > 0.20`)
- **Shows potential** to become A-category with focused effort

### Opportunity List

Displays promising customers with:
- Current sales volume
- Growth percentage
- Current category and gap to reach A
- Suggested actions (e.g., "Increase visit frequency", "Introduce premium products")

<Tip>
  Growing a B-category customer into A is often easier than acquiring a new customer. These opportunities should be high priority.
</Tip>

## How to Use This View

### Monthly Portfolio Review
1. **Check Gini index trend** — Is concentration increasing or decreasing?
2. **Review at-risk list** — Schedule visits for high-value flagged customers
3. **Analyze new/lost customers** — Are we growing or shrinking the base?

### Strategic Account Planning
1. **Identify category A customers** — Ensure they have dedicated relationship management
2. **Review scatter plot** — Find high-frequency/low-ticket customers for upselling
3. **Check concentration** — If >85% revenue from A customers, diversify

### Sales Team Coaching
1. **Filter by salesperson** — Show each rep their at-risk accounts
2. **Use opportunity list** — Assign B-category growth targets
3. **Compare Pareto distribution** — Which salespeople have healthiest portfolios?

## KPI Calculation Details

**Customer KPIs** calculated by `kpi_clientes(df, cli_df, df_ant, fecha_hasta)`:

```python
# Aggregate by customer
kcli = df.groupby('id_cliente').agg({
    'importe_neto': 'sum',
    'fecha': 'count',  # Frequency
    'fecha': 'max'     # Last purchase date
}).reset_index()

# Calculate derived metrics
kcli['ticket_promedio'] = kcli['importe_total'] / kcli['frecuencia_compra']
kcli['dias_desde_ultima_compra'] = (fecha_hasta - kcli['fecha_ultima_compra']).dt.days

# Assign Pareto categories
kcli_sorted = kcli.sort_values('importe_total', ascending=False)
kcli_sorted['pct_acumulado'] = kcli_sorted['importe_total'].cumsum() / kcli_sorted['importe_total'].sum()
kcli_sorted['categoria_pareto'] = kcli_sorted['pct_acumulado'].apply(
    lambda x: 'A' if x <= 0.80 else 'B' if x <= 0.95 else 'C'
)

# Compare vs previous period
kcli_ant = df_ant.groupby('id_cliente')['importe_neto'].sum()
kcli['vs_periodo_anterior'] = (
    (kcli['importe_total'] - kcli_ant) / kcli_ant
).fillna(0)
```

**Pareto concentration** calculated by `get_concentracion_pareto(kcli, "importe_total")` in `src/pareto.py`
