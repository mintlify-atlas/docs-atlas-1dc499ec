## Overview

The Product Analytics view helps you understand which products drive revenue, identify slow-moving inventory, and optimize your product mix. Using visual treemaps and Pareto analysis, you can make data-driven decisions about pricing, promotions, and inventory.

<Note>
  This view answers: "Which products actually generate revenue, and which are sitting on shelves?"
</Note>

## Key Metrics

The top KPI cards provide portfolio overview:

### Active SKUs
- Total number of products flagged as active in `productos_df`
- Calculated as `prod_df[prod_df['activo'] == True]['id_producto'].nunique()`
- Represents your current catalog size

### Product Categories
- Number of distinct categories in your portfolio
- From `productos_df['categoria'].nunique()`
- Examples: Beverages, Snacks, Dairy, Cleaning, etc.

### Products Without Movement
- SKUs with no sales in the last 60 days
- Indicates potential dead inventory
- Warning flag if count is high relative to total SKUs

**Detection logic:**
```python
ultimo_venta = df.groupby('id_producto')['fecha'].max()
corte_60 = fecha_hasta - timedelta(days=60)
prod_sin_mov = prod_activos[
    ~prod_activos.isin(ultimo_venta.index) | 
    (ultimo_venta < corte_60)
]
```

### Average Sales per SKU
- Mean revenue per product: `total_sales / active_sku_count`
- Helps benchmark individual product performance
- Identifies if revenue is concentrated or distributed

<Tip>
  If 10-15% of SKUs have no movement in 60 days, that's normal. If it's >25%, you may have an inventory management issue.
</Tip>

## Product Treemap

A visual hierarchy showing products sized by revenue:

- **Rectangles:** Individual products
- **Size:** Proportional to sales volume (larger = higher revenue)
- **Color:** By category for quick identification
- **Hierarchy:** Grouped by category > individual SKU

The treemap makes it instantly obvious:
- Which products dominate your sales
- How categories compare to each other  
- If any products are underperforming relative to space

**Generated using** `chart_treemap_productos(kp, title="Productos por Categoría — Tamaño = Ventas")`

<Note>

</Note>

## Product Pareto Chart

Visualizes the 80/20 principle in your product catalog:

- **Blue bars:** Individual product revenue (sorted descending)
- **Orange line:** Cumulative percentage
- Shows top 30 products by default

**Key insights from Pareto:**
- How many products generate 80% of revenue?
- Is your product mix healthy or concentrated?
- Which specific SKUs are in the critical top tier?

**Generated using** `chart_pareto(kp, titulo="Pareto de Productos", col_nombre="descripcion", col_valor="importe_total", top_n=30)`

Typically, you'll see:
- **A-category (top 20%):** Drive ~80% of revenue — protect availability at all costs
- **B-category (next 30%):** Contribute ~15% — optimize inventory levels
- **C-category (bottom 50%):** Represent ~5% — consider discontinuation or clearance

## Products Without Movement

A dedicated section highlighting inventory concerns:

### 60-Day No-Sale List

Shows products with zero sales in the last 60 days:

- Product name and category
- Date of last sale
- Days since last transaction
- Inventory status (if available)
- Suggested action

**Why 60 days?** Most B2B distribution businesses expect products to move at least once per quarter. 60 days is a warning threshold before it becomes dead stock.

### Recommended Actions

## High-Growth Products

Identifies products with momentum:

### Growth Criteria

Products shown in this section have:
- **Sales increase >10%** vs. previous period: `vs_periodo_anterior > 0.10`
- **Significant volume** (not just one-off sales)
- **Upward trend** over multiple months

### Growth Product List

Displays promising SKUs with:
- Product name and category
- Current period sales
- Growth percentage (e.g., "+45%")
- Suggested actions (e.g., "Increase inventory", "Expand to more channels")

<Tip>
  High-growth products are your best opportunity to gain market share. Ensure you maintain inventory availability and consider promotional support.
</Tip>

## KPI Calculation Details

**Product KPIs** calculated by `kpi_productos(df, df_ant)`:

```python
# Aggregate by product
kp = df.groupby('id_producto').agg({
    'importe_neto': 'sum',
    'cantidad': 'sum',  # if quantity field exists
    'fecha': 'count',   # transaction frequency
    'fecha': 'max'      # last sale date
}).reset_index()

# Join with product master data
kp = kp.merge(
    productos_df[['id_producto', 'descripcion', 'categoria', 'activo']],
    on='id_producto',
    how='left'
)

# Calculate derived metrics
kp['ticket_promedio'] = kp['importe_total'] / kp['transacciones']
kp['dias_sin_venta'] = (fecha_hasta - kp['fecha_ultima_venta']).dt.days

# Assign Pareto categories
kp_sorted = kp.sort_values('importe_total', ascending=False)
kp_sorted['pct_acumulado'] = (
    kp_sorted['importe_total'].cumsum() / kp_sorted['importe_total'].sum()
)
kp_sorted['categoria_pareto'] = kp_sorted['pct_acumulado'].apply(
    lambda x: 'A' if x <= 0.80 else 'B' if x <= 0.95 else 'C'
)

# Compare vs previous period
kp_ant = df_ant.groupby('id_producto')['importe_neto'].sum()
kp['vs_periodo_anterior'] = (
    (kp['importe_total'] - kp_ant) / kp_ant
).fillna(0)
```

## Advanced Analysis

### Product Affinity

If you want to see which products are frequently purchased together:

1. Filter to a specific customer segment
2. Export the transaction data  
3. Use market basket analysis tools to identify bundles

### Price Elasticity

Track how product sales respond to price changes:

1. Note the date of price adjustments
2. Use the evolution chart to see volume impact
3. Calculate elasticity: `% change in volume / % change in price`

### Seasonal Patterns

Use the 6-month evolution to identify:
- **Recurring peaks:** Stock up before seasonal demand
- **Predictable lows:** Reduce inventory during slow periods
- **Anomalies:** Investigate unexpected spikes or drops

<Note>
  For advanced analytics like affinity analysis and forecasting, consider exporting the data to specialized tools or building custom analysis in the system.
</Note>
