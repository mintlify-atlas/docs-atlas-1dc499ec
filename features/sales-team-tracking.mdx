## Overview

The Sales Team Tracking view provides each salesperson with their own personalized dashboard. Salespeople can see their performance, track their quota, understand their position in the team, and analyze their customer and product portfolio.

<Note>
  This view eliminates the need for salespeople to wait for external reports — they get real-time access to their performance data with context on how they're performing vs. the team.
</Note>

## Business Value

<Check>**Self-service analytics** — Salespeople access their own data instantly</Check>
<Check>**Transparent performance** — Clear visibility into quota achievement and ranking</Check>
<Check>**Portfolio insights** — See which customers and products drive their results</Check>

## Personal KPI Cards

### Period Sales
- Total revenue generated: `sum(importe_neto)` for this salesperson
- Delta vs. equivalent previous period (e.g., "+12.3%")
- Personal monthly/period objective from `objetivos_df`
- Fulfillment percentage with traffic light indicator

### Quota Fulfillment
- Large percentage display showing `(actual / objective) * 100`
- Target line at 100%
- Visual gauge chart (see below)
- Updated in real-time based on filter selections

### Customers Visited
- Count of unique `id_cliente` in the period
- Indicates portfolio coverage and activity level
- Calculated as `df[df['id_vendedor'] == vendedor_id]['id_cliente'].nunique()`

### Average Ticket
- Mean sale amount: `total_revenue / transaction_count`
- Helps identify if the salesperson is maximizing order sizes

<Tip>
  A low ticket average combined with high customer count suggests opportunity to increase order size through upselling.
</Tip>

## Performance Gauge

A visual gauge displays quota achievement:

- **Needle position:** Actual fulfillment percentage
- **Color zones:**
  - 🟢 Green (90-100%+): On track or exceeding
  - 🟡 Yellow (70-90%): Approaching quota
  - 🔴 Red (0-70%): Below expectations
- **Generated using** `chart_gauge_cumplimiento(valor=cumplimiento_pct, label="Mi Cumplimiento")`

## Personal Evolution Chart

Track the salesperson's individual sales trend over the last 6 months:

- **Blue line:** Actual monthly sales
- **Red dotted line:** Monthly personal objectives
- **Calculated using** `kpi_evolucion_mensual(df, obj_df, vendedor_id, n_meses=6)`
- Hover to see exact amounts per month

<Note>
  The evolution chart helps salespeople identify their own patterns — e.g., "I always have a strong Q1 but slow Q2."
</Note>

## Team Ranking Position

Salespeople see where they rank among the entire team:

### Position Card
- **Current ranking:** e.g., "#5 of 12"
- **Movement indicator:** 
  - ⬆️ Improved vs. last period
  - ⬇️ Declined vs. last period
  - ➡️ Same position
- **Position change:** e.g., "+2 positions" or "-1 position"

### Full Team Ranking Table
- Shows all salespeople ranked by sales volume
- **Highlights current user's row** for easy identification
- See who's ahead and behind to understand competitive position
- Calculated from `kpi_por_vendedor(df_eq, obj_df, df_eq_ant)` where `df_eq` includes entire team

<Tip>
  The ranking is based on total sales in the selected period. Use filters to see rankings for specific time ranges or channels.
</Tip>

## At-Risk Customers

A dedicated section highlights customers requiring attention:

<Warning>
  Customers flagged as at-risk haven't purchased in 30+ days or show a significant sales decline vs. previous period.
</Warning>

For each at-risk customer, the system shows:
- Customer name and contact details
- Days since last purchase
- Previous purchase frequency
- Suggested action (e.g., "Schedule visit within 7 days")

**Risk detection logic:**
```python
flag_en_riesgo = (
    (dias_desde_ultima_compra > 30) | 
    (vs_periodo_anterior < -0.30)
)
```

<Tip>
  Prioritize A and B category customers in the at-risk list — losing a high-value customer has the biggest impact on your quota.
</Tip>

## How to Use This View

### Daily Quick Check
1. **Check fulfillment gauge** — Am I on track for quota?
2. **Review at-risk customers** — Who needs a follow-up call today?
3. **Monitor ranking** — Did I move up or down this week?

### Weekly Planning
1. **Analyze evolution chart** — Is my trend positive or negative?
2. **Review customer frequency** — Who haven't I visited recently?
3. **Check product mix** — Am I selling the full portfolio?

### Monthly Review
1. **Compare vs. objective** — Will I hit quota this month?
2. **Identify gaps** — Which customer segments am I weak in?
3. **Plan next month** — What actions will improve my ranking?

## Filtering Your Data

Salespeople can use sidebar filters to:

- **Date Range:** Analyze specific periods (This Month, Last Quarter, etc.)
- **Channels:** See performance by sales channel if they serve multiple
- **Zones:** If they cover multiple territories, break down by zone

<Note>
  Filters apply to all KPIs on the page. For example, filtering to "Last Month" + "Retail Channel" shows only retail sales from last month.
</Note>

## KPI Calculation Details

**Personal KPIs** are calculated using the same engine as team metrics but filtered to the specific salesperson:

```python
# Filter all data to this salesperson
df_personal = get_filtered_data(
    fecha_desde, fecha_hasta, 
    [vendedor_id],  # Single salesperson
    zonas_sel, canales_sel
)

# Calculate individual metrics
kv = kpi_ventas_periodo(df_personal, df_ant, obj_df, fecha_desde, fecha_hasta)
```

**Ranking calculation** compares the individual against the full team:

```python
# Get all salespeople performance
kv_all = kpi_por_vendedor(df_equipo, obj_df, df_eq_ant)

# Sort by total sales descending
kv_all['ranking_actual'] = kv_all['importe_total'].rank(ascending=False)

# Find this salesperson's position
mi_ranking = kv_all[kv_all['id_vendedor'] == vendedor_id]['ranking_actual']
```
